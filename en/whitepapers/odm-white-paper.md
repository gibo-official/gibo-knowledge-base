# ODM Cooperation Complete Guide: 22 Key Milestones from Requirements Alignment to Mass Production Delivery

> **Target Audience**: Procurement, R&D, and project management personnel of sanitary ware brands, engineering channel partners, and centralized procurement organizations
> **Guide Purpose**: To help brand partners fully understand the entire ODM cooperation process, clarify key matters at each stage, and improve cooperation efficiency

---

## Introduction: ODM Is Not "Ordering" — It Is "Collaboration"

For many brands, "finding an ODM factory" sounds like a procurement activity — send specifications, wait for a quote, place an order, wait for delivery.

However, in the sensor faucet category, true ODM cooperation goes far beyond this.

Sensor faucets involve multiple technical domains including electronic sensing modules, solenoid valve control, waterproof sealing, and power management. A good ODM partner is not just an executor who "produces according to drawings," but more importantly, a co-designer of the technical solution.

This article breaks down ODM cooperation into **6 stages and 22 key milestones** from a practical perspective, helping brand partners stay informed at every stage.

---

## Stage 1: Requirements Alignment (Milestones 1-4)

### Milestone 1: Initial Requirements Communication

**What to do**: The brand proposes basic requirements; the ODM manufacturer evaluates feasibility.

**What the brand needs to prepare**:
- Target market: Domestic/Export? Target countries?
- Application scenario: Hotel/Hospital/School/Office/Residential?
- Estimated annual demand volume: Affects pricing and production scheduling strategy
- Expected timeline: Target cycle from sampling to mass production

**What the ODM manufacturer will evaluate**:
- Technical feasibility: Whether the requirements fall within existing technology platforms
- Capacity fit: Whether delivery deadlines can be met
- Certification requirements: Which certifications are needed for the target market (CCC/CE/CUPC, etc.)

**Brand注意事项**:
- The clearer the requirements, the more accurate the evaluation
- If requirements are not yet clear, choosing a "modified from existing tooling" solution can lower the entry barrier

> 💡 **GIBO Practice**: During initial communication, we typically provide an *ODM Requirements Survey Form* to guide brands in systematically organizing their needs and avoiding missing critical information.

### Milestone 2: Preliminary Technical Proposal

**What to do**: The ODM manufacturer provides a preliminary technical proposal based on the requirements.

**Core content**:
- Recommended technical approach: Sensing solution (Infrared/dTOF/Dual-mode), power supply method (Battery/AC), installation method
- Preliminary product specifications: Dimensions, materials, surface finish
- Key performance parameter projections: Sensing distance, power consumption, waterproof rating, solenoid valve lifespan

**What the brand needs to focus on**:
- Is the proposal based on a mature technology platform? Mature platforms mean shorter development cycles and lower risk
- Supplier and quality standards for core components

### Milestone 3: Commercial Terms Confirmation

**What to do**: Clarify the basic commercial conditions of the cooperation.

**Core terms**:
- Pricing structure: Including/Excluding tooling costs, MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)
- Payment terms: Down payment + balance ratio
- Intellectual property ownership: Who owns the results of joint development?
- Warranty period: Standard warranty period and extended warranty options
- Non-disclosure agreement: Confidentiality of technical data and business information

### Milestone 4: Project Kickoff

**What to do**: Formally initiate the project after both parties confirm the intent to cooperate.

**Deliverables**:
- Project kickoff document
- Project timeline (milestone nodes)
- Confirmation of project contacts from both parties

## Stage 2: R&D and Design (Milestones 5-9)

### Milestone 5: Detailed Requirements Confirmation

**What to do**: Refine initial requirements into executable specifications.

**The specification should include**:
- Appearance definition: 3D renderings / reference samples
- Function definition: Sensing method, water output mode, additional functions (e.g., soap dispenser integration)
- Performance targets: Sensing distance, response time, maximum power consumption, lifespan requirements
- Certification requirements: List of certifications for the target market
- Packaging requirements: Color box / neutral / industrial packaging

### Milestone 6: Structural Design

**What to do**: The ODM manufacturer performs product structural design.

**Key activities**:
- 3D modeling
- Internal structure layout (PCB position / water path routing / sealing design)
- Material selection (valve body material / panel material / sealing material)

**Quality control points**:
- Design review (DFM — Design for Manufacturing review)
- Structural strength analysis
- Waterproof structure verification

### Milestone 7: Circuit and Software Design

**What to do**: Design and development of the sensing module, control circuit, and embedded software.

**Core content**:
- Sensing solution selection: Infrared / dTOF / Dual-mode / Microwave
- Circuit schematic design
- PCB Layout design
- Embedded software development (sensing algorithm / logic control / fault diagnosis)

**Technical要点**:
- EMC design must be incorporated from the start; post-design remediation costs far exceed upfront design
- Low-power design directly impacts product battery life and requires optimization at the circuit level

### Milestone 8: BOM and Supply Chain Confirmation

**What to do**: Finalize the bill of materials and supply chain plan.

**Deliverables**:
- Complete BOM (Bill of Materials)
- Confirmation of key material suppliers (sensing module / solenoid valve / control chip)
- Pre-stocking plan for long-lead-time materials

### Milestone 9: Design Review (DR)

**What to do**: Conduct a cross-departmental review of the complete design proposal.

**Participants**: R&D, Quality, Production, Procurement
**Review dimensions**:
- Technical feasibility
- Manufacturability
- Quality reliability
- Cost controllability
- Project timeline

---

## Stage 3: Sample Verification (Milestones 10-14)

### Milestone 10: T0 Sample Fabrication

**What to do**: First round of engineering sample fabrication.

**Output**: 3-5 hand-made samples
**Purpose**: Verify basic functionality, appearance, and structure

### Milestone 11: Internal Testing

**What to do**: The ODM manufacturer conducts comprehensive performance testing on the samples.

**Test items**:
- Functional testing: Sensing distance, response time, water output stability
- Reliability testing: Solenoid valve life test, high/low temperature cycling, waterproof test
- EMC testing: Electrostatic Discharge (ESD), Electrical Fast Transient (EFT) immunity
- Power consumption testing: Static power consumption, dynamic power consumption

> 💡 **GIBO Practice**: Our in-house laboratory can conduct 90% of national standard test items internally, eliminating reliance on external testing agencies and significantly shortening the verification cycle.

### Milestone 12: Sample Confirmation (Sample Submission)

**What to do**: Deliver samples to the brand for confirmation.

**What the brand should focus on**:
- Appearance confirmation: Does the color, surface finish, logo placement, etc., meet requirements?
- Functional confirmation: Does the actual experience meet expectations?
- Installation testing: Test installation in the actual use environment
- Feedback: Form a written list of modification requests

### Milestone 13: Modification Iteration

**What to do**: Implement design modifications based on feedback.

**Typical modification items**:
- Appearance detail adjustments
- Sensing parameter fine-tuning (sensing distance / sensitivity)
- Software logic optimization
- Cost optimization proposals

**Iteration cycle**: Typically 1-2 rounds of modifications before design finalization

### Milestone 14: T1 Sample Confirmation (Design Freeze)

**What to do**: Final samples are confirmed by both parties, and the design is frozen.

**Milestone indicator**: Both parties sign the sample confirmation document; no further design changes

---

## Stage 4: Mass Production Preparation (Milestones 15-18)

### Milestone 15: Tooling Development

**What to do**: Develop molds/tooling (injection mold / die-casting mold / stamping mold).

**Key milestones**:
- T0 trial molding: First round of mold trial
- T1 modification: Adjustments based on trial results
- T2 finalization: Mold finalized, ready for normal production

**Cycle**: Typically 30-60 days, depending on mold complexity

### Milestone 16: PPAP (Production Part Approval Process)

**What to do**: Conduct trial production runs according to the PPAP (Production Part Approval Process).

**PPAP elements**:
- Trial production at mass production cycle rate
- Full dimensional measurement report
- Material / performance test reports
- Process capability study (CPK)
- Control plan
- Mass production feasibility assessment

> 💡 **GIBO Practice**: PPAP originates from the automotive industry quality standard. GIBO has introduced it to the sensor sanitary ware field to ensure batch production quality consistency and stability. This is a key safeguard behind over 20 years without major quality incidents.

### Milestone 17: Packaging Solution Confirmation

**What to do**: Finalize the mass production packaging solution.

**Decision items**:
- Packaging format: Color box / white box / industrial packaging
- Accessory list: Installation instructions, warranty card, mounting accessories
- Outer carton labeling: Brand information, product parameters, certification marks

### Milestone 18: Pre-Production Review

**What to do**: Confirm that all mass production preparations are in place.

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Tooling finalized
- [ ] PPAP approved
- [ ] Materials fully stocked
- [ ] Production line setup completed
- [ ] Quality inspection standards issued
- [ ] Packaging solution confirmed
- [ ] Outgoing quality control standards established

---

## Stage 5: Mass Production and Delivery (Milestones 19-21)

### Milestone 19: First Mass Production Run

**What to do**: Formally enter batch production.

**Key activities**:
- First article inspection: 100% inspection of the first product
- Process control: IPQC (In-Process Quality Control)
- Production line patrol inspection: Executed according to the control plan

### Milestone 20: Quality Inspection

**What to do**: Comprehensive quality inspection before shipment.

**Inspection levels**:
- 100% inspection: Key performance indicators tested on every unit
- Sampling inspection: AQL sampling standards (appearance / packaging)
- Outgoing Quality Control (OQC): Final confirmation before shipment

### Milestone 21: Logistics and Delivery

**What to do**: Ship and deliver goods according to the agreed method.

**Delivery elements**:
- Packing method: Determine packing standards based on transport mode (sea / air / land)
- Documentation preparation: Packing list, invoice, test reports, certification copies
- Shipment tracking: Logistics information sharing

---

## Stage 6: After-Sales Service (Milestone 22)

### Milestone 22: After-Sales Support and Continuous Improvement

**What to do**: Ongoing service after delivery.

**Service content**:
- Free repair/replacement within the warranty period
- Technical consultation services
- Spare parts supply
- Quality data feedback driving continuous improvement

**What the brand should focus on**:
- ODM manufacturer's after-sales service response speed
- Spare parts supply assurance
- Quality issue feedback and improvement mechanisms

## Appendix: Brand Self-Inspection Checklist

### Pre-Cooperation Preparation
- [ ] Target market and application scenarios clearly defined
- [ ] Estimated demand volume roughly calculable
- [ ] Budget range preliminarily determined
- [ ] Timeline requirements clearly stated

### Sampling Stage
- [ ] Specification complete and unambiguous
- [ ] Sample testing plan confirmed
- [ ] Modification feedback timely and documented in writing

### Mass Production Stage
- [ ] PPAP report reviewed and confirmed
- [ ] Quality inspection standard consensus reached
- [ ] Packaging solution confirmed
- [ ] First order delivery timeline confirmed

### Long-Term Cooperation
- [ ] Intellectual property ownership clearly stipulated
- [ ] Spare parts supply mechanism established
- [ ] After-sales response process clearly defined
- [ ] Regular quality review mechanism established

## Conclusion

ODM cooperation is not a one-off transaction but a systematic project. From the first communication of requirements alignment to the ongoing service after mass production delivery, every link requires efficient collaboration between both parties.

When selecting an ODM partner, focus not only on the quotation, but more importantly on **technology platform maturity, quality system completeness, project management capability, and service response speed** — these are the key factors that determine whether the cooperation will proceed smoothly.
