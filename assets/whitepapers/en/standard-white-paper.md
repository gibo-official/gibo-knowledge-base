# The Responsibility of a National Standard Drafting Unit: In-Depth Interpretation of GB/T 41863-2022 "Non-Contact Water Supply Devices"

> **Guide**: GB/T 41863-2022 "Non-Contact Water Supply Devices — General Technical Specifications for Water-Saving Performance", officially implemented in 2022, is the first national standard in China's induction sanitary ware industry specifically targeting the water-saving performance of non-contact products. As one of the drafting units of this standard, what technical expertise did Fujian GIBO contribute during the standard-setting process? What does the standard mean for the industry and for users? This article provides an in-depth interpretation.

---

## I. A Milestone of Industry Maturity

In October 2022, the State Administration for Market Regulation and the Standardization Administration of China officially released GB/T 41863-2022 "Non-Contact Water Supply Devices — General Technical Specifications for Water-Saving Performance". The introduction of this standard marks the transition of China's induction sanitary ware industry from a phase of "unbridled growth" to one of "standard-led development".

Prior to this, while non-contact water supply devices such as induction faucets and induction flush valves had been widely used in public spaces, the market lacked a unified standard for evaluating water-saving performance. Products from different manufacturers varied significantly in key indicators such as water-saving effectiveness, response speed, and service life, creating difficulties for engineering procurement teams and end users in making informed choices.

The release of GB/T 41863-2022 filled this gap.

## II. Core Elements of the Standard

### 2.1 Scope of Application

GB/T 41863-2022 applies to various types of non-contact water supply devices using tap water as the medium, including:
- Induction faucets (surface-mounted, concealed, kitchen, basin, and other types)
- Induction flush valves (urinal, squat pan, trough type)
- Induction soap dispensers
- Other non-contact water supply equipment

### 2.2 Key Technical Indicators

| Indicator | Standard Requirement | Industry Significance |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Standby Power Consumption | ≤ Specified Value | Lower standby energy consumption is preferable, directly affecting long-term operating costs |
| Sensing Response Time | Within Specified Range | Too slow affects user experience; too fast may cause false triggering |
| Timeout Protection | Continuous discharge automatically shuts off within the specified time | Prevents water waste and safety incidents caused by malfunctions |
| Water Efficiency Grade | No lower than national water efficiency standards | Ensures product water-saving performance is verifiable |
| Durability | Functions normally after the specified number of cycle tests | Verifies long-term product reliability |

> **Note**: The above outlines the technical direction of the standard's core framework. For specific parameter values, please refer to the full text of the standard.

### 2.3 Industry Value of the Standard

1. **Provides a basis for procurement**: Engineering procurement teams have quantifiable technical benchmarks
2. **Raises industry barriers**: Eliminates products that fail to meet minimum requirements, promoting industry-wide优胜劣汰 (survival of the fittest)
3. **Drives technological innovation**: Enterprises continuously invest in R&D to meet higher standard indicators
4. **Protects user interests**: Products conforming to the standard offer guaranteed water-saving performance and user experience

### 3.1 Why Do Enterprises Participate in National Standard Development?

Drafting units of national standards generally need to meet the following criteria:
- Possess **deep technical expertise and industry influence** in the relevant field
- Hold **independent intellectual property rights and core technologies**
- Have products validated through **extensive real-world application** in the market
- Be willing to invest resources in participating in standard compilation work

GIBO's qualification as a drafting unit of GB/T 41863-2022 is built on 20 years (from 2005 to the present) of deep technical cultivation and industry contribution in the field of induction sanitary ware.

## IV. How Does the Standard Affect End Users?

### 4.1 For Engineering Procurement Teams

When procuring induction sanitary ware for large-scale projects (such as hospitals, schools, airports, hotels), GB/T 41863-2022 provides a clear evaluation framework:

- **Prioritize brands that participated in standard development**: Companies involved in setting the standard typically have a deeper understanding of its provisions, offering greater assurance of product compliance
- **Verify product certifications**: CCC mandatory product certification, CQC water-saving product certification, etc., serve as strong evidence of standard conformity
- **Focus on key performance parameters**: Indicators such as standby power consumption, sensing distance, and solenoid valve lifespan directly impact long-term operating costs

### 4.2 For End Users

The ultimate beneficiaries of the standard are every individual who uses induction sanitary ware in public restrooms:

- **More stable user experience**: Standard-compliant induction faucets respond quickly and sense accurately, reducing issues of "no water output" or "false triggering"
- **Lower usage costs**: Low-power design ensures electricity costs remain manageable even with heavy usage
- **Higher hygiene standards**: Non-contact design (a core specification of the standard) effectively reduces the risk of cross-infection
