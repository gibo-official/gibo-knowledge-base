## 1. Introduction: The Role of Standards in Product Quality and Market Access

Standards are the invisible architecture of industrial quality. For sensor sanitary ware — a product category that combines electronic sensing, hydraulic control, and water efficiency — robust standards serve three critical functions:

- **They define a quality baseline.** Standards establish minimum performance thresholds for static power consumption, response time, durability, and water efficiency, ensuring that every certified product meets a consistent level of reliability.
- **They enable market access.** Public procurement in hospitals, airports, schools, and government buildings in China increasingly requires compliance with specific national standards. Internationally, CE marking, CUPC certification, and NSF registration are prerequisites for entry into the European and North American markets.
- **They drive technological advancement.** As standards evolve, they push manufacturers to invest in R&D, adopt better materials, and refine production processes — benefiting the entire supply chain from OEM/ODM partners to end users.

China's sensor sanitary ware industry has undergone a significant transformation in this regard. From a period of fragmented, company-specific quality benchmarks, the sector has matured into a standardized ecosystem anchored by the landmark national standard **GB/T 41863-2022** and complemented by group standards, international certifications, and continuous revision cycles.

This white paper provides a comprehensive overview of the standards landscape, the key technical indicators that define quality, and what it means for OEM/ODM buyers seeking reliable partners in China's sensor sanitary ware industry.

## 2. Overview of China's Sensor Sanitary Ware Standard System

China's standard system for sensor sanitary ware operates on multiple levels, forming a pyramid of increasing specificity and authority:

| Standard Level | Description | Examples Relevant to Sensor Sanitary Ware |
|:--------------:|-------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **National Standards (GB/T)** | Mandatory or recommended standards issued by the Standardization Administration of China (SAC) | GB/T 41863-2022 — Touchless water-saving equipment |
| **Group Standards (T/)** | Industry-led standards developed by trade associations, often more specific than national standards | T/XMBK 002-2024 — Sensor faucets |
| **Urban Construction Standards (CJ/T)** | Standards for municipal infrastructure products | CJ/T 194-2014 — Electronic faucets |
| **Compulsory Certification (CCC)** | Mandatory product certification for certain categories | CCC for hand dryers and related electrical products |
| **Water Efficiency Labeling** | Mandatory water efficiency rating system | National water efficiency standards for sanitary ware |

The most significant development in recent years has been the publication of **GB/T 41863-2022**, China's first national standard specifically dedicated to the water-saving performance of touchless water-supply devices. This standard fills a critical gap, as previous standards addressed either general faucet performance (without the touchless dimension) or electronic safety (without water efficiency metrics).

Complementing the national standard, the group standard **T/XMBK 002-2024** provides industry-specific technical requirements for sensor faucets, covering aspects such as sensing accuracy, response timing, and environmental durability in greater detail than the broader national standard.

## 3. Deep Dive: GB/T 41863-2022

### 3.1 Overview and Publication

**GB/T 41863-2022** — *Non-contact water supply devices: General technical specifications for water-saving performance* — was officially published in October 2022 by the State Administration for Market Regulation and the Standardization Administration of China. It is the first Chinese national standard dedicated exclusively to the water-saving performance of touchless (sensor-activated) water supply devices.

### 3.2 Scope

The standard applies to all types of non-contact water supply devices that use tap water as the medium, including:

- Sensor faucets (deck-mount, wall-mount, kitchen, lavatory, and commercial types)
- Sensor flush valves (urinal, squat pan, and trough-type)
- Sensor soap dispensers
- Other non-contact water-supply equipment

### 3.3 Key Technical Indicators

The standard defines measurable performance criteria across multiple dimensions:

| Indicator | Standard Requirement | Industry Significance |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Standby Power Consumption** | As specified in the standard (manufacturers must meet defined thresholds) | Lower standby consumption directly reduces long-term operating costs, especially in battery-powered installations |
| **Sensor Response Time** | Within a defined range | Too slow: poor user experience. Too fast: risk of false triggering and water waste |
| **Timeout Protection** | Automatic shut-off after a specified continuous flow duration | Prevents water waste and property damage in the event of sensor failure or obstruction |
| **Water Efficiency Rating** | Must meet or exceed national water efficiency standards | Ensures the product contributes to national water conservation goals |
| **Durability** | Functional integrity after a specified number of cycle tests | Validates long-term reliability and mechanical lifespan |

### 3.4 Industry Impact

The publication of GB/T 41863-2022 has had a transformative effect on the sensor sanitary ware industry:

1. **Procurement benchmarking** — Engineering procurement teams now have a quantified technical framework for comparing products from different manufacturers. The standard is referenced in public tenders for hospitals, airports, schools, and government buildings.

2. **Market consolidation** — Products that cannot meet the minimum requirements are progressively eliminated from the market, raising the overall quality baseline.

3. **Innovation stimulus** — Manufacturers are incentivized to invest in lower-power electronics, more reliable sensors, and longer-life solenoid valves to not only meet but exceed the standard's requirements.

4. **Consumer protection** — End users benefit from consistent water-saving performance and reliable user experience, regardless of which compliant brand they ultimately use.

### 3.5 The 2026 Revision

In April 2026, the first systematic revision seminar for GB/T 41863 was convened in Fuzhou, hosted in the headquarters city of one of the standard's drafting companies. The revision discussion focused on:

- Incorporating new sensing technologies such as dTOF (direct Time-of-Flight) laser sensing
- Defining stricter energy efficiency tier classifications
- Establishing a standardization framework for smart/connected features
- Harmonizing with international standards including EN 15091 and ASME A112.18.1

This revision demonstrates that GB/T 41863 is not a static document but a living standard that evolves with technology and market needs.

## 4. Group Standard: T/XMBK 002-2024 — Sensor Faucets

### 4.1 Background

Published in 2024, **T/XMBK 002-2024** is a group standard specifically addressing sensor faucets (*感应式水嘴*). Group standards in China are developed by industry associations to address specific product categories in greater detail than national standards can provide. They often serve as precursors to future national standards.

### 4.2 Key Areas of Coverage

While GB/T 41863-2022 focuses on water-saving performance across all touchless water-supply devices, T/XMBK 002-2024 provides more granular technical specifications for the sensor faucet category, including:

- **Sensing accuracy and detection range** — Defining the acceptable detection zone for different installation scenarios
- **Response time thresholds** — Both activation and deactivation response times
- **Environmental adaptability** — Performance under varying water pressure, temperature, and humidity conditions
- **Mechanical endurance** — Cycle testing for faucet bodies and solenoid valves beyond the general requirements
- **Electrical safety and EMC** — More specific requirements for electromagnetic compatibility in the sensor faucet context

### 4.3 Relationship to GB/T 41863-2022

T/XMBK 002-2024 operates as a complementary standard to GB/T 41863-2022. Where the national standard sets the broad framework and minimum requirements, the group standard adds product-specific depth. Together, they form a comprehensive regulatory package for sensor faucets in the Chinese market.

## 8. The Future of Sensor Sanitary Ware Standards

### 8.1 Emerging Technology Drivers

Several technological trends are shaping the next generation of sensor sanitary ware standards:

**Laser sensing (dTOF)**: Direct Time-of-Flight laser sensing offers greater accuracy, longer detection range, and immunity to ambient light interference compared to traditional infrared sensing. The 2026 GB/T 41863 revision is already considering the inclusion of dTOF technology parameters.

**Smart connectivity**: IoT-enabled sensor faucets that report usage data, detect leaks, and enable remote configuration are entering the market. Standards bodies are beginning to develop frameworks for data security, interoperability, and connectivity reliability.

**Energy harvesting**: Self-powered sensor faucets that generate operating power from water flow or ambient light are in development, potentially redefining standby power requirements.

### 8.2 International Harmonization

China's standards ecosystem is increasingly aligning with international benchmarks. The 2026 GB/T 41863 revision explicitly considers harmonization with:

- **EN 15091** (European standard for electronic plumbing fixtures)
- **ASME A112.18.1** (American standard for supply fittings)
- **IEC** frameworks for electronic safety and EMC

For ODM clients, this trend means that a product designed to Chinese national standards will increasingly be compatible with certification pathways in other major markets.

### 8.3 Stricter Environmental Requirements

Water scarcity and energy efficiency are driving progressively tighter standards:

- More granular water efficiency tier classifications
- Lower standby power thresholds (approaching the theoretical minimum)
- Extended durability requirements (moving toward lifetime product reliability metrics)
- Recycled material content and end-of-life recyclability requirements
